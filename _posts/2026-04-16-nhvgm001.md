Let me tell you about a webtoon.

Published on a certain Korean platform under the tags 'shounen manhwa' and 'hunter genre,' this webtoon achieved historic, blockbuster success and went on to write a brand-new legend in the Korean webtoon industry.

Its name was 'Underworld'!

It was an absolute sensation. The cast was so incredibly compelling that I ended up binge-reading <Underworld> over five times, all just to catch another glimpse of my favourite character.

But that was the extent of it.

Twenty years had passed since I died in an accident and was reborn into the world of the webtoon 〈Underworld〉.

When I first realized I had reincarnated into the world of a manhwa, I felt like a middle schooler who was convinced they had a black flame dragon sealed within their right hand. However, despite those revelations, my elementary, middle, and high school life proceeded without a hitch.

I did practically have a heart attack when my baby brother, born shortly after my reincarnation, was named Kwon Seowoo, the exact same name as the original protagonist, but even that shock soon faded from my mind.

The original protagonist was an only child raised in a harmonious family. My brother, Kwon Seowoo, on the other hand, was orphaned at sixteen.

I concluded there was no way they could be the same person.

Besides, the big events in these superpower battle series always kicked off before the main characters even took off their high school uniforms. Since I’d already made it to college unscathed, I figured I was already in the clear.

That was why I believed my life’s genre was slice-of-life.

"Aaaaagh!"

"Help me! Please, anyone!"

"Stop pushing!!"

I swear, right up until the moment I checked my bank account this morning and saw my latest paycheck had cleared, I genuinely thought I was living in a slice-of-life.

“……”

With an ear-piercing crash, the department store’s chandelier, which looked like it had cost an obscene amount of money, shattered into pieces.

How much did that thing even cost?

I looked up, dazed, as pillars and walls crumbled right in front of me.

The area was swarming with monsters letting out grotesque shrieks, and through a massive, gaping hole in the wall, the city centre could be seen.

Far below, amid the throngs of people screaming as they stared up at the building, a colossal electronic billboard flickered brightly.

It displayed the face of Baek Gyeon, the undisputed Number One Hunter in the country, but no one had the presence of mind to care about that right now.

"Are there any hunters here? No one?! A gate just opened inside the department store...!"

I could hear the muffled sounds of people screaming and bolting out of the building.

"Wow... this is insane..."

Staring at the surreal, dreamlike scene before me, my voice came out dazed.

Logically, I knew I should run after them, but there was a problem.

"Noona!"

"Ah..."

"Noona, are you okay?! Stay right there! I'll come over to..."

I gave a few tugs at my leg, which was pinned beneath a wrecked pillar. Breaking into a cold sweat, I lifted my head.

There, sprawled in the distance, was my annoying family register mate, reaching a hand towards me. The same one who, just moments ago, had been begging me to buy him all sorts of gaming consoles.

To make matters worse, that was the exact moment the ceiling gave way with a deafening rumble, caving in right before my eyes.

"Kwon Asol!"

Kwon Seowoo, his face twisted in desperation as he shouted his sister’s name, vanished behind a mountain of debris.

My bloodstained lips parted in hollow defeat.

A gate had erupted inside the department store, monsters were gushing out of it, and the building was collapsing in real time. And I was trapped right in the dead centre of this chaos, my foot pinned beneath debris.

When you laid it all out like that, even I couldn't ignore the writing on the wall.

"I guess I'm actually going to die..."

In a world overrun by awakeners, being born as a mere civilian with absolutely zero combat power meant I had to work incredibly hard just to pull my own weight among all those high-flying geniuses.

No, scratch that, ‘hard’ was an understatement. I fought tooth and nail.

&nbsp

*“A warm Americano, coming right up.”*

*"'Coming right up'?! Is that how you address a paying customer?! Get me your manager, right now!"*

*"My deepest apologies, sir. I bring you your warm Americano, dear customer~"*

*"Hello. I've been watching you since last week, and..."*

*"Yes, sir. The restrooms are right out the door and to your right. Have a good day~"*

&nbsp

Maybe it was because I was right about to die? All those entitled customers—the ones I had to plaster on a fake corporate smile for and bow down to, all for a few measly pennies—suddenly flashed through my mind.

The injustice of it all practically brought tears to my eyes.

For the past few years, I had been the undisputed golden child to every convenience store manager in the neighbourhood. I was a max-level, three-year veteran barista at the local Starbucks. I was the absolute first person on speed dial for any friend who needed someone to cover their shift.

In short, despite reincarnating into a hunter-genre world, I had lived the most ordinary civilian life imaginable.

And now, just because I decided to treat my brother to some new clothes for the first time in a while, I was going to be brutally slaughtered in a sudden gate break.

I couldn't have had worse luck even if I tried.

"...⁎⁎⁎⁎ ⁎⁎⁎⁎ ⁎⁎⁎⁎[^1]. Like hell I'm going to die here."

Faced with the sudden terror of death, tears reflexively welled up in my eyes.

I raised my trembling arms and shoved against the rubble in one last desperate attempt, even though it refused to budge no matter how hard I strained.

I didn't want to die. I wanted to live.

To live, and to make it out of here...

And right at that exact moment.

*Shatter!*

"Ah!"

With a sharp splinter of glass, a pitch-black silhouette came flying through the broken window.

"...What did I f⁎cking say. I told you we should've taken Shin Dorim along, didn't I?"

「He’s tied up with family business right now. You can handle something like this on your own, can't you?」

I heard the man mutter into his earpiece as he landed some distance away from me, a loud crash accompanying his descent.

And the moment I finally caught a proper glimpse of his face, I couldn't help but turn my head, my expression stiff.

I had seen this scene somewhere before.

A collapsing department store. The injured protagonist, Kwon Seowoo. And a grey-haired man whose personality was so rotten you could smell it from a hundred meters away.

"Cleanup's done on my end. The second Joo Semin gets back to you, put a tail on Na Jin. That bastard couldn't have gotten far."

Baek Gyeon.

The heir apparent to the Baek Clan, one of the five great families serving as the pillars of the 〈Underworld〉 universe.

He was a ludicrously overpowered character. Despite his staggering prowess as the current number one hunter and his author-certified, drop-dead gorgeous looks, his notoriously atrocious attitude meant he only possessed either rabid super-fans or diehard haters.

*'...Wait, no way?'*

The long-forgotten plot of 〈Underworld〉’s very first chapter suddenly flashed through my mind.

The Second Gate Terror Incident.

It was the incident that kicked off the original story of 〈Underworld〉. The exact incident where an entire department store was reduced to rubble, all due to a villain named 'Na Jin' intentionally unleashing his gate ability.

Caught in the crossfire of this unprecedented, out-of-control gate break, the protagonist of 〈Underworld〉, Kwon Seowoo, experiences a late awakening and becomes an ability user.

The chaotic, unchecked energy radiating from the artificially torn gate had synced up perfectly with Kwon Seowoo’s innate physical constitution—that was the canonical explanation.

Wait a second. What was the protagonist Kwon Seowoo wearing in chapter one? Was it a black tracksuit?

...Wasn't Kwon Seowoo wearing that exact same outfit today?

Right on cue, Baek Gyeon’s earpiece buzzed again. As he irritably jabbed the receive button, his face twisted into a scowl.

"What the hell are you talking about? You're saying a late awakener showed up in the midst of all this?"

Due to the distance, I couldn't catch the entire conversation, but watching Baek Gyeon's expression rapidly crumple in real time was enough to confirm my suspicions.

Kwon Seowoo.

The protagonist of the webtoon 〈Underworld〉. The typical, righteous, kind-hearted shounen protagonist, the type you’d expect from boys his age. One who, after getting swept up in a terrorist attack orchestrated by Na Jin, miraculously awakened as an ability user.

*'...So, what you're telling me is that Kwon Seowoo is *that* Kwon Seowoo?'*

"Send me the coordinates. I'm heading over."

Scowling in deep irritation, the man kicked aside the monster that had been creeping towards him.

Squelch. With a rather foreign sound, the monster’s torso was instantly cleaved in two and sent hurtling through the air.

Only then did the chaos around me, which I’d momentarily forgotten, come rushing back into focus.

Blood, blood everywhere. The moment I became aware of it, the stench hit me like a wall.

My mind was reeling, but my first priority was getting the hell out of here. Just before Baek Gyeon completely turned his back to leave, I desperately shrilled, "E-excuse me!"

"What the... There was someone else here?"

The man who’d been swinging his leg around in the middle of the disaster with zero sense of danger finally turned his gaze toward me. Baek Gyeon's eyes slightly widened.

It felt as if he could paralyse me with just his stare. Before I even knew it, the towering man was suddenly looming over me, close enough that I could practically reach out and touch him.

"Y-you're Hunter Baek Gyeon, right? My leg is pinned under this pillar, and I can't move. Could you please help me out?"

No matter how rotten his personality, a hunter was still a hunter.

Surely he wouldn't just turn a blind eye to a civilian begging for help. And, thankfully, Baek Gyeon did exactly as I asked.

"Ack!"

...Of course, his method wasn't exactly soft. In fact, it was rather brutal.

Using the exact same posture he’d just used to punt the monster, Baek Gyeon simply swung his foot and kicked the pillar of rubble off my leg.

The crushing pressure around my calf vanished, and the debris arced through the air before slamming into the wall and crumbling completely.

*'Wow, I thought he was about to kick me.'*

Thankfully, it seemed my leg had only been wedged tightly between the gaps in the debris. Aside from a slight numbness, I had no trouble moving it.

A tiny tear of relief pricked at my eyes.

"T-thank you. Thank you so much! Luckily, it seems like I'm not injured. Well then, I'll be taking my leave now."

I scrambled to my feet, intending to vanish from his line of sight.

Now, I needed to get the hell out of here before his horridness decided to start something because I dared to ruin his mood or whatever...

"I think I just found something."

「What?」

"No, not the one you just picked up."

I blinked. And the next thing I knew, I was dangling in mid-air, hoisted up by the scruff of my neck by Baek Gyeon.

The only thing supporting my body was his one hand. I thought, what on earth is going on right now?

Why is the number one hunter holding me like a ragdoll?

"I'm bringing it over, so send me the coordinates. ...Instead of wasting time asking if I'm sure, just take a look for yourself. You'll know when you see it."

"Sorry? Huh?"

"Excuse me for a moment."

"Pardon?"

"Hold still."

"W-why are you doing this?! I'm a civilian..."

I instinctively began thrashing around, only for a massive hand to clamp down on the back of my neck. Baek Gyeon hauled me over his shoulder and mumbled, "...B? No, maybe even A-class?"

"Uh, excuse me! Put me, agh, down...!"

I flailed wildly from where I was slung over Baek Gyeon's shoulder, but he didn't pay me the slightest attention.

In the corner of my vision, the frantic glow of an electronic billboard advertised the fast-approaching deadline for the Hunter Exam applications.

&nbsp

**〈HTV has secured exclusive broadcasting rights for the upcoming 204th Official Hunter Exam! Sponsored by the nation's Number One Guild, 'White Flame,' and the White Flame Group.〉**

&nbsp

In the end, after failing to escape, I was forced to hang there with a vacant expression on my face, being hauled away on the back of the number one hunter.

Twenty years. I spent twenty years of my life thinking I was in a slice-of-life.

*‘...I worked so damn hard, too.’*

Turns out my younger brother was the protagonist of a hunter manhwa infamous for killing its entire cast, whether protagonist or extra.
